# ay25-26-cmp208-biometric-attendance-system

#Title:
Biometric Attendance System

#Problem Statement:
Lecturers at the Sir Arthur Lewis Community College usually consume about five or more minutes of class time to take attendance  or may even have to rush at the end of a session, reducing valuable teaching time, especially when the lecturer is new or not yet familiar with students’ names.

#Project Overview: 
At the Sir Arthur Lewis Community College, lecturers rely on manual methods to record student attendance, a process that often consumes valuable class time and may be rushed at the end of sessions. This reduces instructional time and increases the likelihood of errors, particularly when lecturers are new or unfamiliar with students’ names. This research and design project proposes a Biometric Attendance System to address these challenges. The project aims to examine and design a biometric-based approach to attendance recording that improves efficiency, accuracy, and data reliability. By reducing time spent on attendance and minimizing human error, the proposed system seeks to support more effective teaching and enhance attendance management within the college.

#Project Scope
  Research existing biometric attendance systems to identify suitable biometric methods, best practices, and challenges relevant to an academic environment.
  Evaluate current attendance procedures at SALCC to determine limitations affecting efficiency, accuracy, and instructional time.
  Design a conceptual Biometric Attendance System, including system architecture, process flows, and data handling mechanisms.
  Develop functional and non-functional requirements that define how the proposed system would operate and perform.
  Evaluate economic, legal, ethical, and data privacy considerations related to the use of biometric data in attendance management.
  Assess the technical and operational feasibility, including hardware and software requirements, biometric accuracy, system usability, and user acceptance.
  Test the proposed design through scenario analysis to identify potential risks and limitations (no physical system testing).
  Optimize the proposed system design by refining processes and controls based on research findings.
  Deliver a comprehensive final report documenting the research, analysis, system design, and recommendations for future implementation.
